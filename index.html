Bed Sore Reduction
Medical Alternating Air Mattress Prototype

Iris Man Do, Mohamad Hachicho, Tyler Naus, & Davide Seikaly

Introduction

Bed sores are often caused by pressure sustained on an area of the patient due to the patient’s inability to re-position on the mattress. Our goal is to reduce bed sores by designing a device capable of periodically re-positioning the patient to re-distribute pressure areas.

After initial research and user needs development, we began work on designing a medical alternating pressure mattress to reduce the incidence of bed sores.

User Needs

User Need
Alternate pressure areas
Durable
Re-position user
Safe
Sufficient pressure in static and alternating mode
Support user weight

Prototype

The purpose of the prototype is to give a concept of the alternating air cell mechanism used to re-position the patient and re-distribute pressure areas. The prototype is a scaled down version of a real mattress, and includes an automatic pump, tubing, valves, air cells, and cover. The hydraulic circuit is functional to allow for inflation and deflation of cells. The cells are organized into two sets which can inflate or deflate independently.

We used NX to design individual components and construct an assembly. Using our 3D model as a guide, we wrote a list of parts and estimated cost (bill of materials). With Professor Mundo’s help in ordering and receiving the parts, the physical build was commenced.

Iris sewed a cover in which the air cells would be seated. Each air cell was outfitted with an inlet and outlet tubing connection (i.e., adhesive patch with mating part). The hydraulic circuit was formed via cutting sections of tube to desired length and connecting via T- and elbow valves. Ball valves were used on the outlet side of the circuit to control deflation.
